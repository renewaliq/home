04 / 20 The problem

By the time a renewal shows up "at risk" in the CRM, the decision is already half-made.

68%
of lost accounts showed warning signs 90+ days before non-renewal
3
disconnected systems hold the early signal — CRM, ticketing, and billing

Renewal-sales teams already sit on the signal: declining usage, cooling sentiment in support tickets, slipping SLA compliance. The signal is scattered, not missing.

It's spread across three systems that don't talk to each other, read in full by no single person, and rarely connected until a renewal is already in trouble.

Four reasons good teams still get surprised.

Reactive

Siloed

Delayed

09 / 20 The quantum edge

Some renewal patterns don't separate on a classical kernel.

RenewalIQ maps each account into a quantum feature space via a Quantum Support Vector Machine, run on Qiskit Runtime.

Where classical models see noisy, tangled, nonlinear interactions across a small sample of complex accounts, the quantum kernel can expose a boundary a classical one would miss — the difference between a warning at 90 days and one at 9.

10 / 20 Governance & trust

Drift monitoring

11 / 20 How we position it

RenewalIQ is an Advisor. The renewal manager still decides.

It doesn't send the save offer, and it doesn't own the relationship. It surfaces the risk early, explains why, and recommends a next step — the same judgment call a renewal manager already makes, just with weeks more notice.

Cross-border data handling across regions
